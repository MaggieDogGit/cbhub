Redesign the application UI and information architecture to better support correspondent banking analysis, while preserving current functionality as much as possible.

Core modeling rules:
1. Banking Group is the summary and comparison unit.
2. Legal Entity is the actual provider unit.
3. BICs are attributes of Legal Entity, not a separate hierarchy layer.
4. CB Services are attributes/capabilities of Legal Entity, not a separate hierarchy layer.
5. Market coverage is also tied to Legal Entity.
6. Group-level summaries must be aggregated from underlying legal-entity data.
7. Do not model CB services directly on Banking Group.
8. Do not redesign around BIC as a first-class navigation object.

Objective:
Create a clearer, more intuitive UI structure that reflects the real-world hierarchy and prepares the app for competition benchmarking.

Target navigation:
- Dashboard
- Competition
- Banking Groups
- Legal Entities
- Research

Page responsibilities:

1. Dashboard
- High-level overview of the dataset and recent activity
- Summary cards such as number of banking groups, number of competition-marked banks, legal entities in scope, recent research jobs, and recent updates
- Quick links into Competition, Banking Groups, Legal Entities, and Research

2. Competition
- Placeholder or initial comparison landing page for now
- This page will become the main benchmarking dashboard in the next step
- For now, structure it so it is ready for comparison views, filters, and export actions

3. Banking Groups
- List of banking groups
- Group detail page should show:
  - group identity and metadata
  - competition marker
  - all legal entities belonging to the group
  - aggregated CB services summary derived from legal-entity data
  - aggregated market coverage summary derived from legal-entity data
  - summary counts and simple visual grouping where helpful

4. Legal Entities
- List of legal entities
- Legal entity detail page should show:
  - legal name
  - country
  - entity type
  - parent banking group
  - BICs as attributes of the entity
  - CB services as attributes/capabilities of the entity
  - market coverage as an attribute of the entity
  - any existing evidence/confidence/notes fields if already present

5. Research
- Keep current research and jobs capabilities
- Improve layout clarity if needed, but do not break existing flows

Design goals:
- Make the hierarchy visually obvious: Group = summary, Legal Entity = provider
- Remove or reduce confusing page concepts such as ambiguous “coverage pages” if they duplicate or conflict with the new model
- Ensure drill-down from Group to Legal Entity is easy and intuitive
- Make the app feel more like an analyst tool and less like a generic data registry
- Preserve current data and current behavior where possible

Implementation guidance:
1. Propose and implement the new navigation and page structure
2. Refactor current pages/components to align with the new hierarchy
3. Ensure group pages aggregate from legal-entity data rather than storing separate group-level services
4. Ensure BICs and CB services are shown only as legal-entity attributes/capabilities
5. Keep the UI clean, practical, and oriented toward future competitive benchmarking

Constraints:
- No major backend redesign unless necessary for the UI restructure
- Preserve existing functionality
- Do not change the core database schema unless strictly necessary
- Do not introduce BIC as a separate primary object in the UI
- Do not attach CB services directly to Banking Group
